## Understanding Variance (Forecast vs.

Actuals)

Variance in Pluvo is the difference between what you forecasted and what actually happened. By comparing forecasted values to actual results, you can see how closely your plans align with reality. 

This insight is incredibly valuable for making informed decisions and adjustments. For example, if your expenses were higher than forecasted, you can investigate why and take action to control costs moving forward. 

Regularly tracking variance helps you identify trends, spot potential issues early, and refine your forecasts to improve future accuracy, ultimately driving smarter, data-backed decisions.

There are two main ways to visualize variance in Pluvo:

1. [Variance in Grids](/modelling/features/variance/variance-in-grids) 
2. [Variance in Charts](/modelling/features/variance/variance-in-charts)
