Dimensions are a data type in Pluvo, just like other variables. You can use dimensions to categorize data and perform lookups.

A **dimension** consists of a set of **dimension values**. For example:

* A **Department** dimension might include dimension values: *Engineering, Sales, Product*.
* A **Region** dimension might include dimension values: *US, EMEA, AUS*

<Note>
Before you continue, make note of the difference between *Dimensions* and *Dimensions Values*, it will be important throughout the user manual and app. 
</Note>

You can use dimensions to:

* Categorize rows of data (e.g. expenses by department)
* Create reusable drivers (e.g. headcount by region)
* Build filtered rollups and dynamic metrics

### Creating Dimensions

#### 🛠 Creating a Dimension

Before you can segment your data, you’ll need to create a new **dimension**—a category like `Region`, `Product`, or `Department` that helps you organize values within a variable.

**To create a new dimension:**

1. Click the **dimension icon** on the variable you want to apply a dimension to.
2. At the bottom of the dropdown, click **"+ Create dimension"**.
3. Type the name of your new dimension (e.g. `Region`, `Product`, `Department`) and press **Enter**.
4. Your new dimension will now appear in the list of available dimensions.
5. Click on the dimension you just created to **apply it to the variable**.

Now that you’ve created and applied your dimension, you’ll need to add dimension values to begin entering or modeling data.

### Creating Dimension Values

Once you've created a dimension, you’ll need to define its **dimension values**—these are the individual items that exist within that category.

For example, if your dimension is `Region`, the dimension values might be:

* US
* EMEA
* APAC
* LATAM

**To add a new dimension value:**

1. Open the **Dimensions** dropdown.
2. Click on the dimension you want to edit.
3. Scroll to the bottom and click **"Create value"**.
4. Type in the name of the new dimension value and press **Enter**.

![](/images/modelling/t0NMLg7njkeDRu91Ufov.png)

You can add as many values as you need, and they’ll instantly become available across your models.

### Applying & Filtering Dimensions

Once you've created your dimensions and their associated values, you're ready to start applying them to variables in your model.

**To apply a dimension to a variable:**

1. Click the **dimension icon** on the row of the variable you want to modify.
2. In the **Dimensions** dropdown, click the **“+”** icon in the top right to open the list of available dimensions.
3. Use the **search bar** to find an existing dimension by name.
  
* Or, create a new dimension if needed.
4. Each dimension in the list shows how many dimension values are available.
5. Select a dimension to add it to the **“Break down by”** list for that variable.

![](/images/modelling/YEclIzWJ3HRL7JrmrbGA.png)

**To choose which dimension values to include:**

1. Click into the dimension you just added.
2. Check the boxes next to the **dimension values** you want to display in the grid.
3. **Important:** At least one dimension value must be selected for the dimension to be activated on that variable.
4. Use **“Select all”** if you want to include every available value.
5. Click **Save** to confirm your selections.

You’ll now see the variable broken down by the selected dimension values in the grid.

**Managing Dimensions and Values**

You can rename or delete dimensions and individual dimension values by clicking the **three dots** next to their name in the Dimensions dropdown.

This is helpful for cleaning up your structure as your business evolves.

<Warning>
Note that dimensions created through an integration CANNOT be renamed or deleted in Pluvo. 
</Warning>

### Dimension Hierarchies

When you add **multiple dimensions** to a variable, Pluvo creates a **dimension hierarchy**—a way to organize and break down the data across multiple layers.

The hierarchy determines **how the variable is grouped in the grid**, and follows a **top-down order** based on how dimensions are arranged in the Dimensions menu.

**How it works**

* Variables break down **top to bottom** according to the order of dimensions.
* You can **reorder** the hierarchy by clicking and dragging the handles next to each dimension in the Dimensions dropdown.
* Think of it as:\
  `Show me {variable} grouped by {dimension}, then split by {sub-dimension}, and so on.`

**Example**

Let’s say you’ve added two dimensions to `Employee Salaries`:

1. **Department**
2. **Region**

If you reverse the order—putting `Region` first and `Department` second—it will group salaries by region first, then department within each region:

Use dimension hierarchies to create clear and structured models that mirror how your business operates across teams, geographies, or entities.

### Integration Dimensions

Some dimensions in Pluvo can be pulled directly from your connected systems—like your ERP or accounting software—so you can reference real-world data in your models.

For example:

* `GL Account` from QuickBooks or NetSuite
* `Vendor` from your accounts payable system
* `Department` from your chart of accounts

These integration-powered dimensions are automatically maintained and kept up to date as your source systems sync.\
For instance, if you add a new department in NetSuite, that department will show up in Pluvo without any manual setup.

You can reuse these dimensions across your entire Pluvo workspace—making it easier to categorize, filter, and report consistently.

Read more about how to [connect and use integrations](/modelling/features/integrations).

### What's Next?

In Pluvo, dimensions aren’t just for display. They can be referenced directly in formulas and forecasts to create dynamic, scalable logic. You can even link dimension values across models automatically using a powerful concept called **dimension inheritance**.
